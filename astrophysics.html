Astrophysics

Introduction

Astrophysics is the branch of astronomy focused on understanding the nature and mechanics of celestial objects and the universe as a whole. Through the lenses of physics and mathematics, astrophysics explores phenomena ranging from the life cycles of stars to the mysteries of black holes, galaxies, and dark matter. This field combines theoretical models with observational data to explain how the universe operates on both massive and microscopic scales. By investigating questions about the formation of planets, the origins of the universe, and the potential for extraterrestrial life, astrophysics not only broadens our scientific knowledge but also deepens our sense of connection to the cosmos.
